Shelter

Note: Click on the collapsibles to get more information.

    Homeless shelters provide temporary residence for those that are currently homeless. They provide shelter from the elements and also provide meals.
    If you are experiencing abuse in a shelter, contact one your local homeless coalition. If the abuse persists, contact your local law enforcement.
    If you do not have access to a shelter, make sure to find a location to stay that is suitable for the weather and that you have access to public bathrooms.

Housing & Financing

Note: Click on the collapsibles to get more information.

    There are programs across the country that can help you find residence. Some are country-wide, while other are local to cities or states. To apply for these programs, you can either fill out an online application or make a phone call. Some country-wide programs include:
    - Continuum of Care Homeless Assistance Program.
    - Homeless Assistance - U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development.
    The requirements and applications for unemployment benefits varies from state to state. You will need to go to your state website and apply through their system.
    There is a variety of financial aid programs from the federal government, as well as smaller organizations. Some federal programs include aid for those who are low-income, unemployed, disabled, elderly, or veterans. There are also programs that help supply food to those who need it. A quick search of programs for what you need will help you find the applications needed to receive aid.

Jobs

Note: Click on the collapsibles to get more information.

    A resume is typically only one page and includes an overview of your past work and skills in many areas. When making a resume, be sure to list your education, past work experience, volunteer work, and any special skills you have. Include your name and contact information at the top. It is also good to tailor your resume to fit the job you are applying for.
    When searching for jobs, make sure to look based on your previous experience. If you have no experience, look for an entry level job. There are many online sites where you can search for openings in your area. Internet and computer access can be found at your local libraries.
    When filling out applications, make sure you put down the correct information and contacts. Be sure to emphasize why you would be a good fit for the job and how your past experience will help. Make sure you complete all parts of the application before submitting. It can take a few weeks before getting a call back from employers, so be patient.

    Where to Get Clothes

    There are many organizations geared towards providing those in need with professional clothing for free. Some organizations include:

    Dress for Success.

    Career Gear.

    Ready for Success.

    During your interview you will be required to talk about yourself and your past experiences. Some common topics and questions include:

    Tell me about yourself

    Why should we hire you?

    Why do you want to work here?

    What are your greatest strengths and weaknesses?

    What would others say about you?

    Why can you start?

    Do you have any questions?

    Previous work experience

    Education

    Be prepared to answer some of these questions during your interview. Do not memorize answers, but have a framework of what to say. Emphasize how you will be a good fit for the job and why you want to work there.
    It is best to put down a friend or family members address and contacts for your address and phone number. If that is unavailable you can also put down a shelter address and contacts.

Health Services

Note: Click on the collapsibles to get more information.

    Many people now have access to affordable health care due to the Affordable Care Act. Visit the website:www.healthcare.gov
    Follow the instructions based on your area to sign up.
    Many mental health resources are covered under the Affordable Care Act, such as therapy and medications. There are also many free counseling services online. If you ever feel in danger of hurting yourself or others, call the National Suicide Hotline at 1-800-273-8255.
    If you are unable to be insured under the Affordable Care Act, there are many clinics across the country that are free or have minimal fees. A quick search of clinics in your area can be used to locate them.
